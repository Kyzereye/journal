## 1. New vehicle (car, truck, or SUV)

- **When:** Before the end of May 2026.
- **Fuel economy:** Excellent. Whether gas or hybrid/EV, it has to get excellent fuel economy—I like road trips and want to go far without worry.
- **Size:** 4 doors, plenty of room inside, but not so big that it’s difficult to maneuver. Midsize SUV in mind, but a car or truck with similar features is fine.
- **Amenities:** All the latest—cruise control, heated and cooled seats, leather interior, excellent sound system, excellent monitor screen.
- **Budget:** No price limit. The universe is paying for this.
- **What I give in return:** I take good care of the vehicle. I use it for fun and adventure and to get me where the universe needs me to be—quickly, comfortably, and safely.

### Statement to read and focus on twice daily

>Before May 25, 2026, I am in full possession of a brand-new midsize vehicle that fits my life perfectly. It is paid in full with ease. It gets excellent fuel economy, so every road trip is efficient and worry-free. It has four doors, plenty of room inside, and is easy to maneuver. It has everything I want, including cruise control, heated and cooled seats, leather interior, an excellent sound system, and an advanced monitor system that provides me with the most useful information. It looks visually appealing and stylish. It also has features and qualities I did not know I desired. These pleasant surprises make my driving experience even better. It runs flawlessly, is always dependable, and is ready whenever I am.

>In return, I provide excellent services and products,  I take excellent care of this vehicle and use it with gratitude, fun, and purpose. I use it for adventure and to get me quickly, comfortably, and safely to where the universe needs me to be.


>I have unwavering faith that the Universe is now delievering the most suitable vehical for my needs and desires.  I see this reality now, and I am ready to take the definite actions required to claim it.  


### Ways to focus on the feeling of having it

- **Sensory snapshot (30–60 seconds).** After reading the statement, close your eyes and run through one moment: you’re in the driver’s seat. Feel the leather, the wheel. Hear the door close. See the screen and the road. Notice what you feel in your body. Do this right after each reading.

- **“First drive” scene.** Pick one specific first drive (e.g. a favorite road or road-trip stretch). In your mind, run through it: getting in, starting up, pulling out, cruising. Focus on the emotions—excitement, ease, “this is mine.” Replay that same scene each time.

- **One-word feeling.** Choose one word for how you want to feel with that vehicle (e.g. *free*, *capable*, *relief*, *excited*, *grounded*). After reading the statement, say the word and feel that feeling in your body for 10–20 seconds.

- **Contrast then shift.** Notice how you feel *without* it. Don’t dwell. Then switch: “And now I have it.” Let the feeling change—relief, satisfaction, ease. The shift is the practice.

- **Gratitude-as-if.** After the statement, add 2–3 sentences in present tense: “I’m so glad I have this. I love that it’s roomy and efficient. I’m grateful for this vehicle.” Speak as if it’s already true and sit with the feeling.

- **Physical anchor.** Pick one gesture (e.g. hands as if on the wheel). When you do the twice-daily read, use that gesture and pair it with the feeling of having the vehicle. Over time the gesture can trigger the feeling quickly.

Use one option every time, rotate a few, or combine (e.g. read statement → one-word feeling → 30-second first-drive scene).

## 2. South Pacific trip

- **When:** About 4 months—mid November 2026 to mid or late March 2027.
- **Where:** Australia, New Zealand, Fiji area.
- **Feel:** Full of amazing opportunities for fun and adventure. Meeting awesome people and cultivating long-lasting friendships and memories.
- **Money:** The whole trip pays for itself. How is up to the universe.
- **Context (general):** The kind of trip where I’m doing things like hiking, mountain biking, WW2 history, scuba diving, art and museums—but the exact mix is open. Many parts; keeping it general.
- **Travel:** Efficient and higher class when it makes sense. If riding in a bus with crates of chickens brings an amazing adventure and amazing people, that’s fine too. Open to whatever serves the experience.

### Statement to read and focus on twice daily

>From November 2026 through April 2027, I am on my South Pacific journey; 4+ months across Australia, Tasmania, the Fiji area, and the South Pacific. The trip is full of opportunities for fun and adventure. I meet extraordinary people and build lasting friendships and memories.  All costs are met easily before the trip starts through my business, employment, and the generosity of others. I move through this adventure with hiking, biking, diving, sailing, history, art, and whatever else fits with fun and ease. When it matters, travel is efficient and comfortable.  When the moment calls for it, the experience is wild and memorable. I am grateful for this trip and for every person and moment it brings.

>In return, I give back where I can for those who help me with and along my adventure.  I give my full presence, respect for every place and person I encounter, and a spirit of openness and gratitude on the road. 

>I have unwavering faith that the Universe is now delivering everything this adventure needs inculding but not limited to funds, timing, companions, and the fun and adventurous surprises that belong on the road. I see this reality now, and I am ready to take the definite action required to claim it.


### Ways to focus on the feeling of having it

- **Sensory snapshot (30–60 seconds).** After reading the statement, close your eyes and drop into one moment on the trip: the warmth of the sun, the sound of the ocean or a trail, the sight of a new place. Feel your body there—relaxed, alive, curious. Notice what you feel. Do this right after each reading.

- **“One perfect moment” scene.** Pick one scene you can replay (e.g. cresting a hike with new friends, stepping off a boat, laughing at a table with people you’ve just met). Run through it in your mind with as much sensory detail as you like. Focus on the emotion—alive, connected, free, grateful. Replay that same scene each time.

- **One-word feeling.** Choose one word for how you want to feel on this trip (e.g. *alive*, *free*, *connected*, *curious*, *grateful*, *full*). After reading the statement, say the word and feel it in your body for 10–20 seconds.

- **Contrast then shift.** Notice how you feel *before* the trip or when you think you don’t have it yet. Don’t dwell. Then switch: “And now I’m there. It’s happening.” Let the feeling change—relief, excitement, ease. The shift is the practice.

- **Gratitude-as-if.** After the statement, add 2–3 sentences in present tense: “I’m so glad I’m on this trip. I love the people I’m meeting and the adventures I’m having. I’m grateful for this experience.” Speak as if you’re already there and sit with the feeling.

- **Physical anchor.** Pick one gesture (e.g. arms open, hand on heart, or a relaxed “looking out at the view” posture). When you do the twice-daily read, use that gesture and pair it with the feeling of being on the trip. Over time the gesture can trigger the feeling quickly.

Use one option every time, rotate a few, or combine (e.g. read statement → one-word feeling → 30-second one-perfect-moment scene).
