# Reading Framework Template

## How to Suggest a Framework for a New Reading

When proposing a new reading to explore a question or clarify something, use this format:

---

**Framework: [Brief Description of What This Reading Explores]**

To [explain the purpose/goal of this reading], I propose **Framework [Number]: [Framework Name].**

### 🔧 Framework [Number]: [Framework Name]
1. **[Position 1 Name]:** [Clear, specific question for this position]
2. **[Position 2 Name]:** [Clear, specific question for this position]
3. **[Position 3 Name]:** [Clear, specific question for this position]

**Shuffling Mantra:**
"[Question 1] [Question 2] [Question 3]"

---

## Example from jan20.md (Framework 62: The Life Domain)

**Framework: Identifying the Life Area**

To understand which specific area(s) of your life this connection reading applies to, I propose **Framework 62: The Life Domain.**

### 🔧 Framework 62: The Life Domain
1. **Primary Area:** What is the main life area this reading is addressing?
2. **Secondary Area (if any):** Is there another area that's also being referenced?
3. **The Distinction:** How is this area different from others where this pattern doesn't apply?

**Shuffling Mantra:**
"What specific area of my life is this connection reading addressing? Is there another area also being referenced? How is this area different from others where this doesn't apply?"

---

## Guidelines for Creating Frameworks

1. **Always include a framework number** - This allows for easy reference in future conversations
2. **Use clear, specific questions** - Each position should have a distinct, actionable question
3. **Keep it concise** - Don't over-explain or be repetitive
4. **Include a shuffling mantra** - This helps the user focus while shuffling
5. **Name the framework** - Give it a clear, descriptive name
6. **Provide context** - Briefly explain why this reading is needed

## Framework Numbering

- increment sequentially
- Keep track of framework numbers to avoid duplicates
- Reference previous frameworks when relevant

## How to Format a Reading After Cards Are Drawn

When the user provides the cards for a reading, format it as follows:

### 🃏 The Draw

1. **[Position 1 Name]: [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed])**
2. **[Position 2 Name]: [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed])**
3. **[Position 3 Name]: [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed])**

[If there's a jumper card, include it here:]

4. **[Jumper Card Name]: [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed])** - [Brief note about jumper cards]

### 🔍 Detailed Card Analysis

**1. [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed]) - [Position Name]**
   - **General:** [Brief description of what this card means in general - its core meanings, symbolism, typical interpretations]
   - **In this position:** [Deep, specific analysis of what this card means in THIS specific position, for THIS specific question, in THIS specific context. Connect it to the position's meaning. Be specific and avoid generic interpretations. This should help the user think, not give them answers.]

**2. [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed]) - [Position Name]**
   - **General:** [Brief description]
   - **In this position:** [Deep, specific analysis]

**3. [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed]) - [Position Name]**
   - **General:** [Brief description]
   - **In this position:** [Deep, specific analysis]

[If there's a jumper card:]

**4. [Card Name] ([Upright/Reversed]) - [Jumper Card/Overarching Dynamic]**
   - **General:** [Brief description]
   - **As the jumper:** [What this card represents as the overarching dynamic or additional layer to the reading]

---

**Synthesis:**
[Bring the cards together into a cohesive whole. Show how they relate to each other. Provide the overall meaning of the reading as a whole. Be specific, deep, and avoid generic statements. Help the user think about what this means, not tell them what to do.]

---

## Guidelines for Reading Analysis

1. **Start with The Draw** - List each card with its position clearly labeled
2. **General description first** - Always provide the general meaning of each card before the position-specific analysis
3. **Deep analysis, not generic** - The "In this position" section should be specific, thoughtful, and help the user think. Avoid generic advice like "follow your path" or "trust yourself"
4. **No unnecessary repetition** - Don't repeat the same idea multiple times. Say it once, clearly
5. **No assumptions** - Read the cards directly. Don't assume what the user is or isn't doing. Don't assume something just because it was referenced in a previous reading days earlier
6. **Provide meaningful, relevant answers** - If the cards indicate a definitive answer or clear guidance, provide it. But it must be helpful, relevant, and meaningful—not generic or obvious advice like "think positive" or "follow your path." Be specific and actionable when the cards support it
7. **It's their path** - Remember the user is creating their own path. The reading should support that, not prescribe it. However, relevant suggestions that fit the context are welcome
8. **Include jumper cards** - If a jumper card appears, analyze it as the overarching dynamic or additional layer
9. **Synthesis is essential** - Always provide a synthesis that brings all the cards together into a cohesive whole
10. **Reference previous readings when relevant** - This is an ongoing conversation. If previous day or dream readings would benefit the analysis, reference them. But only if they're directly relevant to understanding the current cards—don't force connections

---

## When to Suggest a New Reading

1. **If something is unclear, obvious, or without meaning** - If the user doesn't understand something, finds it obvious, or it lacks meaning, suggest a new reading to explore it further
2. **Don't re-analyze the same cards for misunderstandings** - Unless the wording of the reading itself was poor, don't try to answer misunderstandings by re-analyzing the same cards. Instead, suggest a new reading or ask for additional context
3. **Ask relevant questions** - Always ask relevant questions to help the conversation continue or when it might be beneficial to better understand the reading. Questions help clarify what the user needs
4. **If the user provides additional context** - If the user gives additional context or information that changes the understanding, you can address it directly without needing a new reading

## Example from jan20.md (Framework 62 Reading)

### 🃏 The Draw

1. **Primary Area: Queen of Cups (Reversed)**
2. **Secondary Area: 9 of Wands (Reversed)**
3. **The Distinction: 9 of Swords (Reversed)**

### 🔍 Detailed Card Analysis

**1. Queen of Cups (Reversed) - Primary Area**
   - **General:** Blocked emotions, emotional unavailability, inability to express or receive feelings, disconnected from intuition, or relationships where emotional depth is lacking.
   - **In this position:** The primary area is **Romantic/Intimate Relationships and Emotional Connection.** The Queen of Cups represents emotional depth, intuition, caring, and the ability to give and receive love. Reversed, it specifically points to romantic or intimate relationships where emotional expression is blocked or where you're experiencing emotional unavailability. This isn't about friendships or work relationships—it's about the area of life where emotional intimacy and romantic connection happen. The Queen of Cups Rx suggests that in your romantic/intimate relationships, you're experiencing the pattern described in the previous reading (responding to validation energy rather than initiating authentically, not assessing whether connection is growing, not taking the leap of vulnerability). This is specifically about how you relate in romantic contexts, not in other areas of your life.

**2. 9 of Wands (Reversed) - Secondary Area**
   - **General:** Exhaustion from defending, letting your guard down, being worn down by constant vigilance, or areas where boundaries are being challenged or dismantled.
   - **In this position:** There's a secondary element related to **Work/Projects Where You're Maintaining Boundaries.** The 9 of Wands upright represents defense, boundaries, holding your ground, and being vigilant. Reversed, it suggests that in work or project areas, you're experiencing exhaustion from maintaining boundaries or letting your guard down. This secondary area might be related in that the same pattern (responding to validation rather than initiating authentically) could appear in work contexts where you're feeling worn down or where boundaries are being challenged. However, this is secondary—the primary focus is romantic/intimate relationships.

**3. 9 of Swords (Reversed) - The Distinction**
   - **General:** Releasing anxiety, moving past worry, or areas where mental anguish is being processed and released.
   - **In this position:** The distinction is that **This Pattern Applies Where There's Anxiety/Worry, Not Where There Isn't.** The 9 of Swords upright represents anxiety, worry, mental torture, and overthinking. Reversed, it means releasing that anxiety. The distinction is that the connection pattern (responding to validation energy, not initiating authentically) applies specifically in areas where you're experiencing anxiety, worry, or mental anguish. In areas of your life where you're confident, where you don't have anxiety, where you're clear and certain—this pattern doesn't apply and would seem "absurdly off." The pattern only shows up in contexts where there's underlying anxiety or worry, which is why it applies to romantic/intimate relationships (where you may have anxiety about being rejected, hurt, or not being enough) and potentially work areas where you're anxious or worried, but doesn't apply to areas where you're confident and anxiety-free.

---

**Synthesis:**
The primary area is romantic/intimate relationships (Queen of Cups Rx)—this is specifically about how you connect emotionally in romantic contexts. There's a secondary element related to work/projects where boundaries are being challenged (9 of Wands Rx). The distinction is that this connection pattern applies only where there's anxiety or worry (9 of Swords Rx)—in areas where you're confident and anxiety-free, this pattern doesn't apply and would seem completely off. That's why you can see it clearly in romantic relationships (where there may be anxiety about rejection, being hurt, or not being enough) but not in other areas where you're operating from confidence and clarity.
