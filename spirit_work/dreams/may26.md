# 📅 05-26
Dream: downhill run in snow, wolves at a distance, half-awake telling

## The dream

You had to go somewhere, and you knew there was danger in going, but you also knew you had to go. You started in the late afternoon with snow all around you, running downhill through it on a trail like a ski slope. There was plenty of open space to run in and trees along the sides. The detail was not strong everywhere, but the motion was clear, and the downhill grade made the running easier than it would have been on flat ground.

At one point you saw yourself running as if from another person's view. You were worried about wolves, and you did see them off in the distance. They were following you, but they stayed far enough back that they were not on you.

The strange part was how the experience came to you in bed. You kept waking, or you may never have fully gone to sleep, and it felt more like you were thinking the story forward than watching a full dream. There was a strong push to record it, as if the subconscious was insisting it mattered. You were wide awake and hoped you could sleep again.

You did get back to sleep, and something continued in the subconscious, but you never returned to the same scene. The first part felt like the part you were supposed to remember. You wrote it down so you would not forget, and then nothing more came.

---

## Analysis

This dream has two threads that belong together, and both are worth keeping even though the second act never returned.

In the story thread you are already committed to a journey you cannot skip, and you accept that danger is part of it. The snow and the ski-slope trail give you speed and room, so movement feels possible, but the trees at the edges and the wolves behind you keep the world from feeling safe. The wolves matter because they are real in the dream but not yet upon you. They turn the run into something you are doing under watch, not something you are doing in pure freedom. Seeing yourself run from another vantage point adds a second kind of watch, because you are both the runner and someone observing the runner, and that split can show up when waking life asks you to move forward while also judging how you look while you move.

In the bed thread the psyche is not only telling a story but also insisting that the opening be carried into waking memory. The half-asleep quality matters because it blurs the line between dream and deliberate thought, and that can happen when the mind is trying to deliver a message without finishing the full scene. You slept again and felt that work continued underground, but you did not re-enter the same images, which often means the first passage was the payload and the rest was never meant to arrive that night.

I would not force a fixed symbol meaning tonight. The scene already has a clear emotional shape: required movement, help from momentum, risk that follows but has not caught you, and pressure to preserve what you were given before it fades. If more returns later, the most useful additions are where you were headed, what the danger felt like in your body, and what the wolves felt like besides fear alone.
