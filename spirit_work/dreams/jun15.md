# 📅 06-15
**The Impala with Laurie: abandoned valleys, wolves, and the driver who will not go**

## The dream

You may have been doing something before you got to the part of the dream you remember. You were in a car that was definitely the car from the dream on June 14 — like a 1959 Impala convertible, a long car, and this time it was a convertible. You were in the passenger seat. Driving was a woman you used to date for a long time. Her name was Laurie.

You were driving along on rural roads through small valleys. There were some pine trees, but for the most part the landscape was open aside from the hillsides of the valleys. There may have been a fire. It was very bright, maybe a cloudy day. At one point you thought you recognized a building that, after you woke up, you think you remember from another dream.

You were talking as you drove along. You do not remember what about, but she was saying something about how pretty it was. It was pretty to her, but you only saw the abandoned buildings. It was a place you knew, although you do not recognize it from waking life. You were pointing out certain things and telling stories about them as you drove, but she was not seeing what you were seeing in the way you were seeing it. You were getting a little frustrated by this. The more you looked, the more abandoned buildings you saw. They seemed to be camouflaged and blended into the landscape.

At one point you told her to pull over and you would show her some stuff. You did, and you were showing her something when you said let's go for a walk around. About that time you realized there was some kind of dog, maybe a wolf, that was stalking you. You were still in the car. You looked up the hillside, now a steep cliff in front of you or maybe up some side canyon, and saw a large piece of rock like a pillar break off and fall. It was far enough away that you were in no danger from any rockslide. Then another wolf appeared.

You told Laurie to drive, to go, but she did not. You were then in the back seat of the car with the door on the passenger side open. Some smaller animal was coming up alongside you, hopping toward you. For some reason you were alarmed by it and kicked it away. Then another started to come toward you and you kicked that one away also. The first one was easy to kick away, but the second one was not as easy. You looked up and saw that the wolves were coming at you but were still a safeish distance away. You repeated again for Laurie to drive, but she was not. On the fourth time you yelled at her to drive, you woke yourself up when you yelled out loud for Laurie to drive.

**Waking note:** Laurie was the only woman from your past that you actively released. In `days/jun14.md` you wrote about releasing attachments to who you used to be. At one point you took an active role: hands together like in prayer, thanked her for the time spent together and what you learned, and with a forward motion and opening of your hands facing up, you released her. There are other women from your past you have not done this with. She is the only one.

## Analysis

### The same car, a different seat

This connects to `spirit_work/dreams/jun14.md` for a concrete reason, not because both nights mention cars. You named the vehicle yourself: a long 1950s Impala-style convertible from the night before. In June 14 you were driving it through a tight urban square, talking to an unseen passenger about scrubbing the floor, and you got about sixty percent around the loop before the dream cut back to cleaning. In June 15 you are in the passenger seat on rural roads while Laurie drives.

The carrier returned, but the role reversed. Your transport dreams often stall before the journey completes. Here the stall is sharper because you are not at the wheel. You can see threat, name direction, tell stories about the landscape, and kick at what hops toward the open door, but the car does not move until your voice breaks into waking. The June 14 car proved you can navigate a hard turn without backing up. The June 15 car proves that skill does not help if someone else holds the wheel and will not go when you say go.

### Laurie and the release you already did

The link to `days/jun14.md` is also direct. You wrote about releasing attachments to past roles and named Laurie as the only woman you formally released with prayer, thanks, and open hands. The dream does not treat her as gone. She is driving, talking, calling the valley pretty, and sitting still when you need motion.

That is not a contradiction unless release means erase. You may have released the claim on her as a living chapter you must keep carrying. The dream may be asking what happens when a released person is still in the vehicle. She is not blocking you from seeing. She is not sharing your view. She is not responding to danger on your timetable. Active release may have freed you from holding on. It did not install a partner who drives when wolves appear.

January's Laurie dream in `spirit_work/dreams/jan11.md` is a different scene. There she was historical ideal used to defend against feeling plain next to others. This night she is present, in motion on rural roads, and wrong about what she sees. The thread across months is Laurie as a high-definition figure from the past, but June 15 is less about beauty as trophy and more about two people in the same car who inhabit different versions of the same place.

### Pretty valley versus abandoned buildings

She sees beauty. You see camouflaged abandonment. The more you look, the more structures blend into the hills until you name them. You tell stories about what you see. She does not see it your way. Your frustration is not mainly romantic. It is epistemic. You are trying to make the visible real to someone who is looking at the same road and reporting a different world.

That argument connects to June 12 without forcing a false rhyme. On the mesa you corrected east while others pointed wrong. Here you correct perception while your driver names the scenery pretty. In both dreams you trust what you see more than the people beside you. June 12 was direction on a map. June 15 is history buried in a landscape that looks fine from the passenger window until you stare long enough to see what is hollow inside the hills.

The abandoned buildings camouflaged in open valleys may belong to the same family as the crust in June 14, but the image is not identical. The crust was a layer on a floor you scrubbed off to reach concrete. Here the structures are part of the scenery until attention reveals them. Old paths, old identities, and old towns of the self can look like ordinary hills until you are the one who keeps noticing the windows. Laurie does not deny your stories. She simply does not see the buildings. The dream may be separating what you have released in ritual from what you still see everywhere in the terrain.

### Pull over, show her, walk around

You stop the car to prove what you see. That is a fair move after frustration in the passenger seat. The dream does not show her conversion. It moves immediately to stalking and cliff. Teaching someone your view does not finish the scene. The walk you propose does not happen. Threat arrives while you are still parked with the person who called the place pretty.

### Wolves, rock fall, and the smaller animals

The wolves stalk at a distance. A pillar of rock breaks from a cliff farther away, still not on you. Danger is real but not immediate at first. That matches several June dreams where threat escalates in stages rather than jumping straight to catastrophe.

The smaller hopping animals at the open door are different. They come close enough that you kick from the back seat. The first is easy. The second is harder. You are defending yourself inside a car that is not moving, with the passenger-side door open, while the larger predators are still far enough to feel safeish. The dream splits threat size. Small alarms you can bat away with effort. Large ones you cannot fight from the seat. You need the car to move.

Yelling for Laurie to drive four times, then waking on the fourth shout, makes your voice the only thing that finally breaks the scene. That connects to `days/jun14.md` for a reason: you wrote that your inner voice is loud, sometimes negative, sometimes the channel through which you work with energy. Here the outer shout does what the driver will not do. It ends the dream. Whether Laurie is Laurie, a spirit figure, or a part of you that sees the pretty valley and freezes, the motor that fails is forward motion, not perception.

### Bright light, fire, and the building you almost know

Very bright light runs through June: washed mesa in June 12, sunny bazaar in June 14, bright cloudy rural fire-scape here. Brightness in your file is not comfort. It is high visibility with thin or harsh feeling. You see more, not less.

The building you think you remember from another dream is worth logging without guessing which file. Recurring architecture in your dreams often marks a place your psyche returns to when the subject is belonging, origin, or old life. Leave the link open until you know which night it was.

## What this dream may be about

You are not driving. You are with the only past partner you formally released, on roads you know in the dream but not in waking, seeing abandoned camouflaged buildings in a valley she calls pretty. You try to show her. Wolves and falling rock announce pressure. Small creatures get close enough to kick. Large ones close the distance more slowly. Laurie does not drive when you ask. You move to the back seat. The door stays open. You kick, you yell four times, and you wake on your own shout.

The dream does not undo your release ritual. It tests what release left in place. You may no longer be holding Laurie as a claim on your future, but you are still beside her in a car that will not leave when the landscape shows what you see and the animals approach. The work may be less about releasing her again and more about learning to move when the driver will not, without waiting for shared sight or a fourth warning.
