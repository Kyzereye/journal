**Meditation Vision: The Woman in the Yellow Dress**

### 🌙 Narrative

You were meditating. A woman popped into your vision. She was about 5'6", black hair, attractive. She was wearing a tiered dress that was yellow with a small pattern—a little bit of red in the pattern. She was standing right in front of you. She had her left hand up about head high to the side of her head, the back of her hand facing you. It seemed like she was frustrated at you, maybe even exasperated. You thought she said something like, *What are you waiting for.* You're not sure what she meant at the time. That's when she disappeared.

---

### 🔍 Analysis

**1. Context: meditation, not sleep**
The image came during meditation—so it's not a dream in the sense of the sleeping psyche sorting the day. It's a **vision that broke into intentional stillness.** Something pushed through while you were receptive. That suggests the content is something the psyche (or whatever you call it) wanted to put in front of you in a moment when you were paying attention.

**2. The woman**
A specific figure: height, black hair, attractive, vivid enough to describe. In a Jungian frame she could be **anima**—the inner feminine that often carries what the conscious mind hasn't fully owned: desire, relationship, the "other," or the part of you that challenges or questions. She could also be a **guide or messenger**—the form the unconscious uses to deliver a message. Or she could point toward the **relationship you want** (you've said you know what kind of girlfriend you want)—the vision putting that in front of you in the form of a woman who is frustrated with you. The key is: she's **in front of you**, **facing you**, and her emotion is directed **at you.**

**3. The dress: yellow, pattern, a little red**
Yellow: light, clarity, mental energy, sometimes caution or warmth. Red: passion, life, urgency, blood. A **small pattern** suggests something to read, detail, a design. So the figure carrying the message is dressed in **clarity/light with a thread of urgency**—not neutral, not cold. The dress doesn't dominate; it frames her. She's the message; the colors underline that there's something clear and something urgent.

**4. The gesture: hand up, back of hand toward you**
Left hand up to the side of her head, back of hand facing you. That's a **classic exasperation gesture**—"I can't even," "what are you doing," a mix of frustration and disbelief. She's not reaching for you; she's **reacting to you.** So the vision is not an invitation in a soft sense; it's a **confrontation.** Something in you (or the psyche) is frustrated with how you're showing up in this moment.

**5. "What are you waiting for"**
She says something like *What are you waiting for.* That's a **direct challenge.** It doesn't explain; it questions. It implies: you're waiting; you shouldn't be; or you're waiting for the wrong thing; or the wait itself is the problem. Given your Feb 19 reading—Page of Swords Rx (curiosity/mental openness blocked), Knight of Swords Rx (charge stalled), and the question *why has it stalled?*—the vision lines up. The reading pointed to a **stall** and a **block.** The woman in the vision is asking: **what are you waiting for?** So the meditation vision and the cards are on the same theme: something is held back; something is waiting; she's asking you to name it or drop it.

**6. She disappeared when you wondered what she meant**
The moment you moved into "what did she mean?" she was gone. So **the message was delivered; the figure left when you shifted into interpretation.** That could mean: the question *is* the message. Don't dissolve it into "what did she mean"—sit with *What are you waiting for?* as the thing that was given. The vision doesn't stick around to explain; it asks and leaves.

---

### 🔧 Synthesis

The vision puts a woman in front of you—frustrated, exasperated, hand up—saying *What are you waiting for*, then disappearing. She's not gentle; she's challenging. Read symbolically: the inner figure that carries relationship, desire, or the part of you that wants motion is **frustrated with the wait.** That fits the reading: direction is clear, you're seeing the resource (friends, help), you're acting—but the Page is blocked and the Knight's charge is stalled. The vision doesn't say *what* to do. It asks: **what are you waiting for?** The question is the message. What you do with it—whether you answer it, sit with it, or let it work—is yours. The woman gave you the question; she didn't stay to hear the answer.
