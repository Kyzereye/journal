**You:** First off, I never said that I was not doing the reading correctly. I was doing them and then felt the various feelings. You made an assumption here that you should not have made. **Assistant:** That assumption was wrong. You were already reading and feeling. The odd part was only the repetition of the same words twice a day, not a missing feeling step.

What Hill and Neville say about stopping is closer to the fuel point you named. Hill’s twice-daily practice is meant to keep the desire impressed until the definite plan is carried through, not only until the first signs appear. Neville said the same thing differently: stay in the state until the outer catches up, including the middle stretch where something has started but is not finished. Early business movement and thoughts about paying for a vehicle can be that first stage. Cutting the practice then can starve the next stage of the same focus and feeling that helped open the door.

The bind is real. Reluctance to start again is one side. Seeing that stopping may have cut the fuel is the other. You do not have to pretend the repetition never felt odd. You also do not have to leave opportunities half-fed because repetition felt odd.

Return because of the fuel link you already see, not as punishment for stopping. Keep doing what you were already doing — read, then feel. If twice a day still feels strange, do once a day rather than zero. Zero is what cuts the supply. Once a day with real feeling is still continuity. Use the sessions to stay with the end state of the opportunities that already started to move, not only to ask for a first sign.

The reluctance may just be not wanting to restart a schedule that felt repetitive even when it worked. That is a reason to resume in a form you will keep, so the fuel does not go out again mid-stream.
