**Assistant:** Part of the point of the trip, yes. Not the whole point. On July 18 you wrote that Belize was mostly about sitting, rain, a cold, and island time, not a big life turn like quitting Merick. On the flight home you saw the money pinch in action: you skipped small things you wanted because you were worried about spending. Both can be true. Worrying about lost years is another form of the same pinch. You already saw the problem clearly. Turning that into guilt about the past keeps your attention on lack instead of on what you spend next.

Wild spending is not the fix, and it is not the balance. The fix is deciding your money rules when you are calm, then following them. Pay the mortgage and basic bills because those are real bills. Paying them does not mean the universe failed you. The pinch shows up in the optional spending: the small Belize skip, holding miles instead of using them, having money for a car and still not buying it.

For the South Pacific, do what was already suggested for Belize in June. While you are calm, write down how much cash you will spend, how you will use miles, and a date by which you decide. Then treat that plan as the yes, and stop checking every day whether you deserve the trip. Slow income right now does not mean you have to put every desire on hold until Dan, Todd, or a membership launch makes you feel safe. It means you stop treating “I do not see big money yet” as a reason to refuse small things you can already afford under your own rules. The mortgage and the written trip limit already cover being careful. The pinch starts when careful turns into worry that follows you into every drink, meal, and side activity.

Getting back on track does not mean fixing the past. It means one next purchase or booking that fits a desire you already planned for, without adding another layer of maximizing points or waiting for a better deal. If the South Pacific numbers do not fit your written limit when you run them honestly, do a smaller trip or set a later date with the same rule. Do not blow the budget, and do not put the trip off forever. Belize already showed you how the pinch works. The next step is to stop refusing the small yeses that already fit the rules you set.
