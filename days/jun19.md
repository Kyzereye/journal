Business class is the sharp example because it is not only about comfort. It is a floor you set after lean years. Economy can feel like going backward — back to the four-year stretch, back to someone who has to grind for every yes. At 59, with a South Pacific adventure written into your desires as months of hiking, diving, and meeting people, cattle class on a long haul is not just a seat. It can read as "this trip is not really mine yet." So the scarcity voice does not only count pennies. It uses business-class math as proof the whole thing is unaffordable, which gives you a respectable reason to stay home.

That is different from your own statement in [shorter-term-desires.md](../moving-forward/shorter-term-desires.md): efficient and higher class when it makes sense, and open to the bus with chickens if the adventure asks for it. Right now the filter is running in one direction only — higher class required, so the trip must be fully funded at the premium level before you allow yourself to want it. The desire file already allows downgrade when the experience is worth it. Scarcity does not.

You said you still need to organize the mindset behind this. Three threads worth separating when you sit with it:

One is identity. Business class may be a line you drew between past-you who had to scrape and present-you who travels differently. The question is whether that line protects you or disqualifies you.

One is control. Optimizing every price gives the illusion of steering a huge unknown trip. If you cannot nail the perfect fare, hotel, and gear deal, you can defer the whole thing while still feeling responsible.

One is the old get-by pattern in travel clothes: find a reason the numbers are not right yet, so you never have to be visible on the road as someone who committed.

You do not need the full mindset sorted today to move. You need to notice when saving money is planning the trip versus when it is hunting for an exit. Belize, Hawaii in the lean years, New York last summer — you went when the structure was mostly there, not when every class of service was perfect. South Pacific can hold business class where the long legs matter and still happen without every subsidiary cost being minimized into paralysis.

When you organize the thoughts behind the mindset, write them here. The trip in November is far enough out that scarcity can run the whole summer unless you name what business class is actually buying you — and what you are willing to trade if the universe offers the adventure before the spreadsheet is flawless.
